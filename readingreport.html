Entry Type A

Passage Response

Choose one sentence or short passage from today's reading that surprised you, confused you, or stayed in your mind. Write it down and explain why you chose it.

Your task Find a passage that made you stop — even briefly. It does not need to be the most important line in the book. It just needs to be the one that affected you most today.

Entry Type B

Prediction & Tension

Identify the biggest unresolved question in your reading — something the book has raised but not yet answered. Then make a specific, reasoned prediction.

Your task Surface readers cannot do this task well. Finding real tension in a text requires you to have read carefully enough to notice what is being withheld — a character's true motive, a conflict that hasn't resolved, an idea that keeps returning without explanation.

Entry Type C

Personal Connection

Connect something specific from today's reading to your own experience, a current event, or another text you know. The connection must be precise and argued, not just mentioned.

Your task The connection must be specific. "This reminds me of my life" is not enough. You must say exactly what in the text connects to exactly what in your experience — and explain why that connection is meaningful, not just obvious.
